COVID-19 Information

What is COVID-19?

Coronaviruses are single-stranded huge RNA viruses that can infect both humans and animals. They usually target the lungs of the infected and can cause mild to fatal diseases like "acute respiratory distress syndrome" or ARDS. There are four categories of coronaviruses, namely: alpha-, beta-, gamma-, and delta-coronaviruses. There are many coronaviruses out there, but the coronavirus people are most familiar with is the "severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2" or SARS-COV-2. SARS-COV-2 caused the virus "coronavirus disease 2019," in short, COVID-19.

COVID-19 is under the subfamily beta-coronavirus. COVID-19 originated on the Huan seafood market located in Wuhan, China. This virus was unknown last December 2019, and it was mistaken for pneumonia. The World Health Organization (WHO) declared COVID-19 as a pandemic on 11 March 2020. A pandemic is defined as an epidemic that spanned across different countries and continents.

What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

There are different symptoms for every patient. Below are some of the symptoms of the infected with COVID-19:

  • Cough
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Body Aches
  • Headaches
  • Loss Of Taste Or Smell
  • Sore Throat
  • Runny Nose
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Besides those symptoms, here are some serious and requires medical attention symptoms:

  • Chest Pains
  • Trouble Breathing
  • Sleepiness
  • Discoloration Of Skin, Lips, and Nails
  • Confusion

How does COVID-19 spread?

COVID-19 can be transmitted through small liquid particles coming from an infected person through their mouth or nose. The virus can spread from coughing, sneezing, talking, or breathing. Even without the symptoms, infected people are still infectious. Close interaction with an infected person increases the chance of being infected as well. A distance of less than 1 meter is considered to be close contact. Crowded locations increase the chances of getting infected because close contacts are inevitable. Poorly ventilated areas are also a way of spreading the virus.

How is COVID-19 different from influenza?

COVID-19 and Influenza are similar in many ways. Both are a virus that targets the lungs. They have similar symptoms, such as cough, fatigue, headache, and stuffy nose. They can range from mild to severe cases. If either of the said diseases is not taken seriously, it can be fatal. The way COVID-19 and Influenza how they infect others share the same characteristics.

Even though they are similar, they still have notable differences. COVID-19 spreads faster and more fatal than Influenza. The cures for both diseases are different even though they are viruses. Antiviral drugs are available to lower the chance of having severe cases and death. However, COVID-19 doesn't have a reliable cure. Severe COVID-19 patients rely on ventilators to assist them in breathing. Moreover, antibiotics don't work on either of the diseases.
